Complete the political awareness exercise here in writing.

Assuming I was 100% correct:
When I was living in a student flat over the summer temporarily, before other students arrived and I would be moving out, I helped them set up the internet. Everything was set up and I ended up moving out of the flat at the end of the summer, as planned. About half-way through the year, one of the students at the flat (I'll call her Abby) who I didn't get along with, told me that they were unsatisfied with the internet, after having a salesperson knock on the door, and they wanted to change to another plan. They wanted me to pay the full $200 disconnection fee because the internet was still under my name (a lesson was definitely learned there), and I had set up internet they thought was unsatisfactory.

I went around to the flat to talk to all the students there. We all gathered in the lounge. Abby was the only one who talked to me and was adamant that I pay the full $200 fee. I felt attacked and none of the other students would back either of us up so I was got the impression that they were ambivalent over the whole situation. I ended up offering a compromise: There were 6 students, so I would pay 2 parts, and they would each pay 1 part each. $25 each and $50 for me. I was still unhappy about being asked to pay for something that I felt didn't concern me at all, but I was glad to reach an agreement where they didn't expect me to pay all $200, especially as they had all the power as the contract was in my name and I would have to spend $200 anyway if they decided that they would stop paying their bills.

In the end, Abby ended up leaving the flat pretty shortly after that, before we got around to changing the contract and none of the others ever contacted me about the internet until they left the house at the end of the year.

My Reflection:
I can see that these stories are extremely similar. I found it hard to write from Abby's perspective because I still feel very bitter about the experience. I helped them get their flat set up and ended up getting bitten for it. I was also stuck with a $100 fee for not returning the router at the end of the contract.
Looking back at the situation I think that our situation arose purely because of how we each perceived me setting up the internet. I thought I was doing them a service by helping them get internet. I guess Abby thought I was looking out for my own needs and not the flat's with the contract/telco I chose. I think if I found myself in this situation again I would still help with choosing the internet but I would make sure it was under a permanent resident's name and not my own.
