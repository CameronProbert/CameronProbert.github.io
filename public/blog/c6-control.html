When programming, what are some of your triggers? What causes a disproportionate emotional response?

I find it so frustrating when code that should compile just doesn't, and then restarting the IDE allows it to compile. I think this annoys me so much because I did everything right, so it's not my fault, but I spent a long time trying to figure out the compilation error.

I also find it frustrating when there is a particular nuance that I haven't understood in something that is causing a problem. In this sprint I had this happen twice while trying to use a for (x in y) loop. It doesn't seem to work exactly as it does in Java. I had to replace both instances of it with for (var i = 0; i < y.length; i++) loops. I think that it may have been iterating over y in a way I wasn't expecting.

How did you find the Siberian North Railroad exercise (Stop, Breathe, Notice, Reflect, Respond)?

I was surprised by how simulating the experience in my mind really made me physically feel the same way as I had in the actual moment. I also found that I could then make the physical feeling disappear by relaxing and concentrating on my breathing.
